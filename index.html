ZONE 1

The heart of the metropolitan area, Zone 1 is characterized by high-density commercial and residential buildings, extensive public transportation networks, and vibrant cultural landmarks.

Key features include the central business district with its iconic skyscrapers, historic neighborhoods dating back to the 19th century, and the largest concentration of museums and theaters in the region.

Population: Approximately 850,000 | Area: 32.5 km² | Density: 26,154/km²

ZONE 2

Zone 2 offers a mix of residential communities with varying density, from high-rise apartments to single-family homes, along with neighborhood commercial centers and community facilities.

This area is known for its excellent school districts, numerous parks and recreational facilities, and a more suburban feel while still being well-connected to the urban core.

Population: Approximately 620,000 | Area: 58.3 km² | Density: 10,635/km²

ZONE 3

Zone 3 contains the majority of the region's manufacturing and logistics facilities, with extensive transportation infrastructure including rail yards, highways, and port facilities.

The area has undergone significant redevelopment in recent years, with former industrial sites being converted to mixed-use developments while maintaining its core manufacturing functions.

Population: Approximately 210,000 | Area: 72.1 km² | Density: 2,912/km²

ZONE 4

Zone 4 is the ecological heart of the region, featuring protected natural areas, agricultural land, and recreational open space that serves as the "lungs" of the metropolitan area.

This zone includes two major river systems, several nature reserves, over 50km of hiking trails, and the region's primary water reservoirs.

Population: Approximately 85,000 | Area: 320.5 km² | Density: 265/km²

ZONE 5

Centered around the flagship state university campus, Zone 5 combines academic facilities with student housing, research parks, and innovation hubs that drive the region's knowledge economy.

The area features a vibrant cultural scene influenced by the university community, with numerous bookstores, cafes, performance venues, and public lectures open to all residents.

Population: Approximately 180,000 (including 45,000 students) | Area: 28.7 km² | Density: 6,272/km²

ZONE 6

Zone 6 encompasses the region's waterfront, including commercial port facilities, recreational beaches, marine research institutes, and coastal residential communities.

This zone is particularly vulnerable to climate change impacts, leading to innovative coastal protection measures and sustainable development practices being pioneered here.

Population: Approximately 310,000 | Area: 95.2 km² (including 22 km² water area) | Density: 4,242/km²

Project overview

Inquiry question

To what extent does the design of this space contribute to positive interactions between human society and the environment?

Objective: To explore the environmental conditions of various urban areas and present the findings through an interactive map.

Hypothesis: The ecological comfort of urban zones is influenced by the amount of greenery, air pollution, and the quality of urban infrastructure.

Format: A website featuring a map that displays data collected from various zones. Users can compare different areas of the city and draw conclusions about their environmental comfort.
